# Grillmi — Grill Timings Reference

Exhaustive, sourced reference for the Grillmi PWA. Each section contains per-cut tables with thickness, doneness, heat zone, grill temperature, per-side time, total time, turn count, rest time, and notes. Source citations use inline tags like `[Weber-DE]`, `[SE]` (Serious Eats), `[Migusto]`, `[BBossi]`, `[Napoleon]`, `[Traeger]`, `[BBCGF]` (BBC Good Food), `[Grillportal]`, `[Meathead]` (AmazingRibs). Full links in the Sources section at the end.

## Assumptions

All numbers below assume:

- **Gas grill or kettle** with a working lid; lid closed except when explicitly noted (e.g. very thin fillets, sausages you baste).
- **Two-zone setup**: one side direct (all burners on, or full charcoal bed), one side indirect (off burner, or no coals underneath). "Direct high" ≈ 230–290 °C grate, "direct medium" ≈ 180–230 °C, "indirect" ≈ 150–180 °C unless stated.
- **Meat tempered to room temperature** — 30–45 min out of the fridge for steaks, 20–30 min for chops, up to 60 min for roasts. Cold meat adds 20–30 % to times and causes uneven cooking. [SE, Meathead]
- **Lightly oiled food, not the grate** — oil on the grate burns and sticks; oil the food. [Weber-DE]
- **Clean, hot grate** — preheated 10–15 min with lid down before anything goes on.
- **Internal temp is the truth**. Time tables are guidance; pull at target core temp using an instant-read probe (Thermapen-class). All core temps below are pull temps — carryover adds 2–5 °C during rest for steaks, 3–7 °C for roasts. [SE, Meathead]
- **Turn count**: "flip once" is the Serious Eats default for steaks ≥ 3 cm; Weber and Migusto increasingly recommend flipping every 45–60 s for a more even crust and cook (Kenji has shown this empirically). Both work — the Grillmi default is **flip once** for cuts ≤ 3 cm, **flip every 60 s** for cuts ≥ 4 cm or for reverse-sear finish.
- **Resting** applies to any cut cooked to medium-rare or above at ≥ 3 cm thickness. Tent loosely with foil; do not wrap tightly (steams the crust).
- **Swiss/DACH cut naming** follows Migusto and Betty Bossi. Where a Swiss cut has no clean US analogue (Hohrücken, Huft, Nierstück) the German term is primary.

| Rare             | 49–52                 | —     | —                | —                   | —           | 43–46 |

Pork note: Swiss and US guidance since 2011 (USDA) allows pork whole-muscle (filet, Schweinshals, chops) at 62–63 °C pull with rest → 65 °C. Older DACH guidance still says 70 °C; Grillmi defaults to 63 °C pull for whole-muscle pork, 70 °C for ground pork and Schweinsbraten. [USDA, Migusto, BBossi]

Poultry note: breast is safe at 70 °C pull + 3 min rest (equivalent to USDA 74 °C instantaneous). Thighs are eaten at 80–85 °C because collagen needs the heat to render. [SE, Meathead]

Two muscles, two cooking speeds — strip side cooks faster than filet side. Orient filet toward the cooler zone.

| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

### Flank steak (Bavette / Flanksteak)

Thin, long, with aggressive grain. Eat at medium-rare, slice thin across the grain or it's chewy.

| Thickness | Doneness    | Heat zone   | Grate temp | Per side | Total    | Turns     | Rest    |
| --------- | ----------- | ----------- | ---------- | -------- | -------- | --------- | ------- |
| 1.5–2 cm  | Medium-rare | Direct high | 260–290 °C | 3–4 min  | 6–8 min  | Flip once | 5–8 min |
| 2–2.5 cm  | Medium-rare | Direct high | 260–290 °C | 4–5 min  | 8–10 min | Flip once | 8 min   |

Sources: [SE] 54 °C internal, 6–10 min total. [Weber-DE] 6–8 min.

Notes:

- Marinades (lime, soy, chipotle, chimichurri) love this cut.
- Slice in ~5 mm strips across the grain — identify the grain direction before cooking, it's obvious.
- Never past medium — collagen doesn't have time to render in a thin steak.

### Skirt steak (Kronfleisch / Nierenzapfen)

Even thinner than flank, even beefier. Hot and fast, period.

| Thickness | Doneness    | Heat zone   | Grate temp | Per side | Total   | Turns     | Rest  |
| --------- | ----------- | ----------- | ---------- | -------- | ------- | --------- | ----- |
| 1–1.5 cm  | Medium-rare | Direct high | 290 °C+    | 2–3 min  | 4–6 min | Flip once | 5 min |
| 1.5–2 cm  | Medium-rare | Direct high | 290 °C+    | 3–4 min  | 6–8 min | Flip once | 5 min |

Sources: [SE] "hottest fire you can manage, 3–4 min total for thin skirt". [Meathead] agrees.

Notes:

- Trim outer silverskin.
- Pat dry — the exterior crust is the whole point.
- Across the grain, thin slices, same as flank.

### Tri-tip (Bürgermeisterstück)

Triangular, grain changes direction mid-cut. Reverse-sear is best.

| Thickness / weight | Doneness | Heat zone | Grate temp | Timing | Total | Turns | Rest |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ~700 g, 4–5 cm thick at center | Medium-rare | Reverse-sear | 130 → 290 °C | 25–35 ind + 2/side sear | 30–45 min | Flip every 60 s direct | 10 min |
| ~1 kg | Medium-rare | Reverse-sear | 130 → 290 °C | 35–45 ind + 2/side sear | 40–55 min | Flip every 60 s direct | 10–15 min |

Sources: [SE] "Santa Maria style" — indirect to 49 °C, sear to 54 °C. [Traeger] 130 °C smoke to 52 °C, sear. [Weber-US] agrees.

Notes:

- Pull at 52 °C for medium-rare (carryover is real on a 1 kg piece).
- Slice thin, watch the grain change at the apex — re-orient for the second half of the roast.

Closer to entrecôte in feel but leaner on one side with a fat strip on the other.

## Open questions / gaps

Items where sources diverge materially or where Grillmi needs a Marco-decision:

- **Pork pull temp — 63 °C (USDA/modern) vs 70 °C (conservative DACH)**. Migusto uses 62–63 °C for whole-muscle pork but Betty Bossi and older Swiss literature still default to 70 °C. Grillmi default picked: **63 °C for whole-muscle, 70 °C for ground/Braten**. Confirm this is OK for a Swiss-first audience, or make it a user-selectable preference in settings.
- **Burger pull temp for home-made patties**. USDA/BLV say 70 °C because grinding contaminates the interior. But a huge share of Migusto-style BBQ culture still cooks medium-rare burgers. Grillmi default picked: **70 °C pull**, with an explicit "home-ground only, my risk" override toggle. Needs UX thinking.
- **Flip-once vs flip-often for steaks**. Both work, research supports frequent flipping as slightly more even. Currently Grillmi defaults: flip-once ≤ 3 cm, flip-every-60s for reverse-sear and thick cuts. Could be simplified.
- **St. Galler Bratwurst mustard debate** — pure tradition, no food-safety question, but if Grillmi shows tips we need a position. Suggest: show neutral note, no advocacy.
- **Horse steak (Pferdesteak)** — Swiss sources only. No Weber, no Serious Eats coverage. Numbers rely on Migusto + Betty Bossi alone; not triangulated. Flag as "Swiss-specialty, single-source" if we surface source confidence.
- **Riesencrevetten timings** — Migusto says 3 min/side, Weber 2 min/side. 50 % disagreement. Picked 2 min/side (Weber matches prawn size vs time better); users who like them firmer can add 30 s.
- **Ribs method (3-2-1 vs unwrapped)** — massive BBQ-forum debate. No single correct answer. Grillmi default picked: **2-2-1 for Baby Back, 3-2-1 for St. Louis**, with unwrapped alternative exposed for purists.
- **Chicken wings** — baking-powder-dry-brined is measurably crispier [Meathead, SE] but adds a prep step. Should Grillmi default to the simple version and show the baking-powder tip, or default to the optimized method? Suggest: simple default, tip available.
- **Whole fish on grates without a basket** — assumes a well-oiled, hot, clean grate. Real-world users will shred the skin. Grillmi should probably recommend a fish basket for trout/dorade and say so clearly.
- **Plant-based patties internal temp** — no authoritative temp guidance from Beyond or Impossible. Picked 65–70 °C "hot through" by analogy to ground-beef safety, but these products contain no raw protein needing pasteurization — this is overkill. Confirm stance.
- **Thick-cut asparagus** — white asparagus timings not well covered in grilling sources; most Swiss sources boil+butter. If Grillmi shows white Spargeln, the parboil+grill method above is one of several credible approaches.
- **Grilltimer-by-Migusto feature parity** — we don't have the full app spec in this doc; need a side-by-side comparison of Migros-app items vs this reference to catch any missing SKUs (e.g. specific marinated Migros product lines like "M-Classic Schweinshals mariniert" with pre-set times).
- **Altitude / wind corrections** — none of the sources give a clean adjustment factor. In a Swiss context (Alps), open-air windy grilling at altitude realistically adds 10–20 % to indirect-cook times. Out of scope for v1 but flag for later.
